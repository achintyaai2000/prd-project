What is a PRD?

The Blueprint Every Product Needs

A Product Requirement Document (PRD) is the single source of truth that a Product Manager writes before a single line of code is typed. It answers the most important question in product development, not how to build something, but what to build and why.

Think of it like an architect's blueprint. Engineers, designers, marketers, and leadership all align around the PRD to ensure everyone is building the same thing, for the same user, with the same goal in mind.

Product Managers at Google, Amazon, Microsoft, Uber and other top tech organizations spend a significant part of their role writing, refining, and defending PRDs. They are the language of product strategy made concrete.

🧭

Alignment

Keeps all teams: engineering, design, marketing, pointing in the same direction.

🚧

Scope Control

Defines what's IN and what's OUT, preventing scope creep from killing timelines.

📐

Structure

Provides a professional framework: problem, users, requirements, metrics.

🔁

Accountability

Documents the WHY behind every decision so teams can revisit it months later.

The Reality of PRDs

Why PRDs Exist
(According to Developers)

Every software team has lived these moments. PRDs exist to prevent chaos.

PM
"Build me a button that makes users happy!"
DEV
"...a button that dispenses pizza?"
🍕

"Make Users Happy"

Without a PRD, "make users happy" becomes 47 different interpretations. One dev builds confetti, another builds a pizza button. The PRD says: increase session time by 20%.

PRD says:
"Add search"
Fuzzy search • Filters • Autocomplete • History • Voice input • Trending queries • Search analytics • A/B testing • Spell check • Synonyms...

Scope Creep Iceberg

Every feature request is an iceberg. The PRD defines exactly which 10% is above water and ships it. Non-Goals are just as important as Goals.

Week 1
"Here's the idea!"
Week 3
"Actually, can we also add—"
Week 6
"One more small thing..."
Week 12
💥 "Why isn't it done yet?!"

The Deadline Paradox

Features without a PRD grow endlessly. A PRD locks scope at Week 1 so Week 12 actually ships, instead of becoming a legend of eternal development.

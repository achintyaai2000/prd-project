Section 02

Executive Summary

Average video lengths on YouTube are increasing significantly, especially for tutorials, podcasts, interviews, and documentaries. Viewers often want specific information rather than watching an entire long video. Currently, scrubbing through long videos is time consuming and frustrating, and viewers frequently rely on comments or descriptions to find important moments. Creators have no structured way to highlight key sections of their content.

This PRD proposes Video Chapters for YouTube, a feature that allows creators to divide their videos into named sections with timestamps, and lets viewers jump directly to those sections via an interactive chapter navigator in the video player.

With Video Chapters, creators can mark key sections (e.g., "Introduction," "Binary Trees," "Battery Life Test"), viewers see a chapter list and can click any chapter to jump to that timestamp, and chapters appear in the progress bar, video description, and potentially in YouTube's search results and recommendations. This feature is designed to improve navigation, reduce abandonment, increase watch completion for long videos, and help creators showcase the full value of their content.

Section 03

Problem Statement

The Core Problem: As video lengths increase, especially for tutorials, podcasts, interviews, and documentaries; viewers face significant friction when trying to find specific information. Users must manually scrub through long videos to find relevant sections, there is no structured navigation to key moments, and viewers rely on unreliable comments or descriptions to locate important content. Creators cannot highlight or organize key sections, leaving valuable content undiscovered.

Who Has This Problem?

  • Learners and students — watch 1–3 hour educational lectures and need to revisit specific topics (e.g., "Binary Trees," "Calculus Chapter 3"); don't remember where a topic starts and must scrub the entire lecture
  • Professionals learning new skills — watch skill based tutorials (e.g., "Learn Python," "Excel for Business") and need specific sections like "Loops" or "Pivot Tables" without watching the full video
  • Information seekers — watch product reviews and want specific sections like "Battery Life" or "Camera Test" without sitting through the entire review
  • Entertainment viewers — listen to 2–4 hour podcasts or watch sports interviews and want to skip to specific topics or highlights without sitting through unrelated content
  • Creators — educational creators, tech reviewers, podcast hosts, and documentary filmmakers with no structured way to highlight key sections, leading to undiscovered valuable content and lower completion rates

Evidence of the Problem:

SignalData Point
Increasing video lengthsAverage lengths rising significantly for tutorials, podcasts, interviews, and documentaries; analytics show higher scrubbing behavior and abandonment for long videos
User feedbackUsers frequently request "timestamp navigation," "section markers," and "chapters for long videos"; support teams observe repeated questions about finding specific parts
Creator feedbackCreators report valuable sections remaining undiscovered, lower viewer satisfaction, and difficulty promoting specific content sections
Competitive contextPlatforms like Spotify, Coursera, and Udemy offer chapter/chapter like navigation for long content; YouTube lacks a native structured chapter system, creating a competitive gap

What Happens If We Don't Solve It?

  • Viewers face increased frustration navigating long videos, spend more time manually searching for content, and are more likely to abandon videos entirely
  • Creators continue to see valuable content sections remain undiscovered, reduced watch completion rates, and difficulty promoting podcasts, courses, and educational videos
  • YouTube faces poorer user experience compared to competitors, reduced engagement on long-form content, and risk of losing users to platforms with better navigation for long content

🚫 Non-Goals (Out of Scope for v1)

  • Auto generated chapters — chapters must be creator defined, not AI generated in v1
  • Chapters in YouTube Shorts — Shorts will not support chapters in v1
  • Interactive chapter previews — hover thumbnails when hovering a chapter marker are out of scope
  • Chapter based recommendations — recommending individual chapters (not just videos) is out of scope
  • Custom chapter styling — creators cannot customize chapter colors or fonts in v1

User Journey & User Stories

Primary User Journey — Creator Flow

Upload video to YouTube → Open YouTube Studio → Select the video → Go to Video Details → Click "Add Chapters" → Enter timestamps (e.g., 0:00, 5:30, 12:45) and chapter titles (e.g., "Introduction," "Binary Trees," "Practice Problems") → See preview of how chapters will appear to viewers → Save and publish → Chapters now visible to all viewers instantly

Viewer Journey

Open long video on YouTube (mobile, web, or desktop) → See chapter markers in the progress bar → See chapter list below the video or in the navigator panel → Click any chapter title → Video jumps to that timestamp instantly → Navigate between chapters freely as needed throughout viewing

Section 08

Functional Requirements

FR-01  P0

Creator Chapter Editor in YouTube Studio

In YouTube Studio, creators must be able to add chapters with timestamps (e.g., 0:00, 5:30) and chapter titles (e.g., "Introduction," "Binary Trees"). The editor must be simple, intuitive, and show a live preview of how chapters will appear to viewers before publishing.

FR-02  P0

Chapter Validation

The system must validate that timestamps are in ascending order, timestamps are within the video duration, and chapter titles are not empty. Invalid chapters must show clear, specific error messages guiding the creator to fix the issue.

FR-03  P0

Chapter Markers in Progress Bar

In the video player, chapter markers must appear as visible indicators in the progress bar. Clicking or tapping a marker jumps the video to that chapter's exact timestamp immediately.

FR-04  P0

Chapter List Navigator

Viewers must see a list of all chapter titles (e.g., below the video or in a dedicated navigator panel). Clicking or tapping any chapter title jumps the video to that timestamp. The list must be visible and accessible without additional navigation steps.

FR-05  P0

Cross Platform Chapter Support

Chapters must work consistently on Mobile (Android and iOS), Web, and Desktop app. The UX must be consistent across all platforms with no critical functionality missing on any supported platform.

FR-06  P0

Chapters in Video Description

Chapters must be auto generated in the video description, showing timestamps and chapter titles. This allows viewers to see and access chapters outside the player, in search results, shared links, and the description panel.

FR-07  P0

Edit / Remove Chapters After Publishing

Creators must be able to edit chapter titles, update timestamps, or remove chapters entirely after publishing. All changes must be reflected immediately for viewers without requiring a video re-upload.

FR-08  P0

Chapter Click Event Tracking

The system must track which chapters are clicked and how often chapters are used per video. This data must feed into creator analytics dashboards and platform level optimization to improve the chapter experience over time.

Section 11

Assumptions & Constraints

Assumptions:

  • Creators will manually add chapters using the YouTube Studio chapter editor
  • Viewers have internet connectivity when watching videos and using chapter navigation
  • All target platforms (Android, iOS, Web, Desktop) support rendering chapter markers in the video progress bar

Constraints:

  • Chapters are creator defined only in v1, no auto-generated chapters
  • YouTube Shorts will not support chapters in v1
  • No custom chapter styling (colors, fonts) in v1
  • No hover thumbnail previews for chapter markers in v1

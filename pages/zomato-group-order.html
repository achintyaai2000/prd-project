Section 02

Executive Summary

Today, many Zomato users place group orders (for teams, families, gatherings, etc.) using a single account and device. That requires the person placing the order to pass their phone around so others can select items, which is time consuming, awkward, and increases friction in the ordering experience.

This PRD proposes Group Order Split Bill: a feature that lets multiple participants join a shared cart from their own devices, add items independently, and complete payment via automated UPI based split settlement. The feature will support common split methods (itemized per person, equal split, and custom shares) and allow the organizer to manage the shared cart.

Section 03

Problem Statement

The Core Problem: Zomato users who want to order food for a group currently have no streamlined way to do so. The organizer must either collect orders one-by-one from each person and manually add them to a single cart, or pass their phone around so others can pick their own items. Both approaches are inefficient and friction heavy, increasing time to place an order, creating privacy concerns, causing confusion about who ordered what, and delaying final checkout.

Who Has This Problem?

  • Team managers and office coordinators — organize food for team lunches, meetings, and celebrations; face time consuming coordination and split confusion
  • Party and event organizers — host gatherings for 8-50+ people; struggle with managing preferences and splitting bills fairly
  • College and school friend groups — order together in hostels and PGs; face friction around money and unequal splits
  • Families and shared households — order for multiple members with different tastes; no easy way to track who ordered what
  • Casual meetups and social gatherings — small friend groups who want quick coordination and transparent billing

Evidence of the Problem:

What Happens If We Don't Solve It?

  • Users continue facing friction in group scenarios, longer ordering times, more errors, disputes over splits
  • Lower conversion and lost revenue as users abandon group orders or split into multiple smaller inefficient orders
  • Competitive disadvantage, competitors who launch a smooth group order experience become the default for team meals and events
  • Increased support overhead, more tickets related to wrong orders, missing items, and payment disputes
  • Missed growth opportunity, no group flow means lost chance to increase AOV and drive social, collaborative engagement

User Journey & User Stories

Primary User Journey - Organizer Flow

Open Zomato → Click "Create Group Order" → Select restaurant & delivery address → System generates unique group link instantly → Share link via WhatsApp / Email / SMS → Participants join and add their own items → Organizer reviews full cart (sees who ordered what) → Choose split method (itemized / equal / custom) → System calculates each person's share including taxes & delivery fee → Trigger UPI payment requests to participants → Participants confirm payment → Order confirmed, everyone receives their own receipt

Participant Flow

Receive group link (WhatsApp / SMS / Email) → Click link → Open shared cart in-app or web → Log in or continue as guest → Add own items to common cart → See who else has joined and what they ordered → Review own items and calculated share → Receive UPI payment request for share → Confirm payment → Receive receipt with own items, own share, and full order summary

Epic 1 — Group Order Creation & Link Sharing

US-01 · P0 — As an organizer, I want to create a group order without asking everyone individually, so that I can reduce coordination time.
US-02 · P0 — As an organizer, I want the group order link to be created instantly after creating the group, so that I can share it quickly.
US-03 · P0 — As an organizer, I want multiple options to share the link (WhatsApp, email, SMS, and others), so that I can reach my group easily.
US-04 · P1 — As an organizer, I want to add a custom message when sharing the link (e.g., event name, time), so that participants understand the context.
US-05 · P0 — As a participant, I want to join the group order by clicking the link, so that I can add my items without confusion.

US-06 · P0 — As a participant, I want to add my own items to a common cart from my own device, so that I don't need to use the organizer's phone.
US-07 · P1 — As a participant, I want to see who has joined the group and what others have ordered (optional visibility), so that I can track the group order.

US-09 · P0 — As an organizer, I want to view the full cart and see who ordered what, so that I can manage the order and avoid conflicts.
Section 08

Functional Requirements

FR-01  P0

Group Order Creation & Link Sharing

Users must be able to create a group order from the app or website and instantly generate a unique group order link. The organizer can share this link via WhatsApp, email, SMS, or other supported channels. The link allows participants to join the group order and access a shared cart.

FR-02  P0

Common Cart with Real Time Synchronization

All participants can add, edit, or remove their own items in a common cart from their own devices. The cart must be synchronized in real time across all participants' devices, showing who joined and what each person has ordered. Visibility is configurable by the organizer.

FR-03  P0

Organizer Cart Management & Finalization

The organizer can view the full cart, see who ordered what, and make final adjustments (remove or modify items) before finalizing the order. The organizer has the authority to confirm and place the order once all participants have added their items.

FR-04  P0

Bill Split Method Selection

The organizer can choose how the bill is split: Itemized split (each person pays for what they ordered), Equal split (all participants split the total equally), or Custom split (organizer manually adjusts shares). The system calculates each participant's share, including taxes, delivery fee, and platform fees.

FR-05  P0

UPI Based Split Payment Flow

Once the split method is chosen, the system triggers UPI payment requests to each participant for their share. Participants confirm payment via their UPI app. The organizer can also pay the entire bill themselves if needed. The system tracks payment status per participant in real time.

FR-06  P0

Receipt & Order Confirmation Per Participant

After the order is confirmed and payment is complete, each participant receives a clear receipt showing: their items, their share, and full order summary (including taxes, delivery fee, platform fees). Participants can also see the order status (preparing, dispatched, delivered) in the app.

FR-07  P1

Group Order Expiry & Link Invalidation

The system defines conditions for group order expiry (e.g., organizer cancels, time limit exceeded, no new participants). When expired, the group link becomes invalid and all participants are notified that the group order is no longer active.

FR-08  P1

Cancellation & Modification Notifications

If the organizer cancels or modifies the order after participants have paid, the system notifies all affected participants. The system handles refunds or adjustments based on Zomato's refund policy.

Design & UX Requirements

Entry Points - Users must discover the feature from multiple natural points:

Entry PointDescription
Home ScreenDedicated "Create Group Order" button or card visible when user has selected a restaurant
Restaurant / Menu Page"Start Group Order" button near "Add to Cart" area — visible only for restaurants that support group orders
Cart Screen"Convert to Group Order" option after items are added, allowing conversion without starting from scratch
Order History"Recreate this group order" button for past group orders — supports repeat team lunches and weekly orders
Participant EntryGroup order link (shared via WhatsApp, SMS, email) opens the shared cart in-app or on web

Tone & Messaging Guidelines:

  • Simple and clear, avoid jargon like "distributed cart" or "multi-party settlement"
  • Friendly and social, emphasize collaboration, not transaction
  • Transparent, clearly communicate how the bill is split and what each person pays
  • Use conversational copy: "Everyone orders their own meal.", "No more passing the phone.", "Bill is split automatically."
Section 11

Assumptions & Constraints

Assumptions:

  • Restaurants participating in the pilot support reasonably large orders (10+ items) without operational issues; non-supporting restaurants can be excluded
  • Most participants in group orders have a UPI enabled bank account and UPI app installed
  • For participants without UPI, the organizer can pay the entire bill as a v1 fallback

Constraints:

  • Single restaurant per group order in v1, cross restaurant group orders not supported due to logistics and billing complexity
  • UPI only split payments in v1; cards, wallets, and Cash on Delivery not supported for split flows initially

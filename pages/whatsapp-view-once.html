View Once

Send images and videos that disappear after the recipient views them once, giving users meaningful control over sensitive content without permanent storage, forwarding, or saving

Section 02

Executive Summary

Every day, millions of WhatsApp users share billions of images and videos across personal chats and groups. Once shared, these media files are typically downloaded to the recipient's device and stored in their gallery or app storage, where they remain accessible indefinitely. This can be deeply problematic when senders want to share sensitive or temporary content they do not want stored permanently.

This PRD proposes the View Once feature for images and videos in WhatsApp chats. With View Once, the sender can mark an image or video such that the recipient can open and view the media only once. After viewing, the media becomes inaccessible in the chat, is replaced with a placeholder, is not saved to the recipient's gallery, cannot be forwarded, and cannot be downloaded.

The sender chooses at the time of sending whether to send media as normal (default, stored indefinitely) or as View Once (seen once, not stored permanently). This feature is designed to give users meaningful control over their sensitive content while maintaining WhatsApp's core principles of privacy, simplicity, and end-to-end encryption.

Section 03

Problem Statement

The Core Problem: Users who want to share confidential, sensitive, or temporary images and videos currently have no way to ensure that these files are not permanently stored on the recipient's device. Once shared, media is downloaded to the recipient's gallery or app storage and remains accessible indefinitely, creating privacy risks, loss of control, storage concerns, and reduced willingness to share.

Who Has This Problem?

  • People sharing identity documents or proof — ID cards, passports, event tickets, bank details shared with landlords or service providers; fear that documents will be saved, copied, or misused
  • Couples and close friends sharing personal moments — private photos of trips or celebrations; risk of content being forwarded to other people or groups without consent
  • Users concerned about gallery clutter — "just for reference" images like product pics or menu photos filling recipients' galleries with files they only needed to glance at
  • Users who want "just glance at this" content — quick visuals like outfit checks or table arrangements meant to be viewed once and discarded
  • Privacy conscious users in sensitive environments — people who have experienced prior misuse of photos, or those in conservative environments where sharing certain images carries real risk

Evidence of the Problem:

SignalData Point
Competitor featuresSnapchat's disappearing media became instantly popular and a key differentiator; Telegram and Discord have also introduced privacy focused media features
App store reviewsSignificant Play Store and App Store reviews explicitly ask for "view once images," "disappearing media," and "option to send images that can't be saved"
User quotes"I don't want my photos saved forever." / "I want to send something private that can't be forwarded." / "My gallery is full of random images I only needed to see once."
Internal support trendsWhatsApp support teams observed repeated concerns about media being saved permanently and fear of forwarding; product teams noted users avoiding certain shares due to privacy concerns

What Happens If We Don't Solve It?

  • Competitive disadvantage, Telegram, Discord, and Snapchat may be perceived as "more private" platforms, driving privacy conscious users to switch
  • Persistent privacy and trust concerns, users continue fearing content misuse, hesitating to share important images, and experiencing anxiety about permanent storage
  • Continued gallery pollution, recipients' devices fill with temporary files they never wanted to keep, creating a negative "storage heavy" perception of WhatsApp
  • Reduced sharing behavior, users send fewer images/videos overall or move to other platforms for private sharing, reducing engagement
  • Missed privacy leadership opportunity, WhatsApp's strong E2EE position goes underutilised without a visible, user facing privacy feature to match it

Section 07

User Journey & User Stories

Primary User Journey — Sender Flow

Open WhatsApp chat (1:1 or group) → Tap camera or attachment icon → Select image or video → See View Once toggle on preview screen → Tap the "1" icon / View Once toggle → UI shows explanation: "This photo/video will be viewable once and then deleted" → Tap Send → Media appears in chat with circular "1" indicator → Optionally see "Opened" status once recipient views it

Recipient Journey — Viewer Flow

Receive message with View Once indicator (circular "1" icon) → Tap to open media → Media opens fullscreen → After closing, media becomes permanently inaccessible → Chat shows placeholder: "View Once media has been opened" → Cannot reopen, download, forward, or screenshot the media → Media never saved to device gallery

Epic 1 - View Once Media Creation & Sending

US-01 · P0 — As a sender, I want to choose whether to send media as normal or View Once, so that I can control how the recipient uses it.
US-02 · P0 — As a sender, I want the View Once option to be easy to find and toggle, so that I can enable it quickly without confusion.
US-03 · P0 — As a sender, I want clear information about what View Once means before sending, so that I understand the limitations.
US-04 · P0 — As a sender, I want to see a clear indicator in the chat that the media is View Once, so that I know it was sent correctly.
US-05 · P1 — As a sender, I want to optionally see when the recipient opens the View Once media (e.g., "Opened"), so that I know it was viewed.

Epic 2 - View Once Media Reception & Viewing

US-06 · P0 — As a recipient, I want to see a clear indicator that the media is View Once before opening, so that I understand the limitations.
US-07 · P0 — As a recipient, I want to open and view the View Once media with a clear UI, so that I can see it properly.
US-08 · P0 — As a recipient, I want the media to become inaccessible after viewing, so that I know it was viewed once.
US-09 · P0 — As a recipient, I want a placeholder to appear after viewing (e.g., "View Once media has been opened"), so that I know the media is no longer accessible.
US-10 · P0 — As a recipient, I want to be able to see who sent the View Once media, so that I know the source.
Section 08

Functional Requirements

FR-01  P0

View Once Media Selection & Toggle

When a user selects an image or video to send in a chat (1:1 or group), the preview screen must show a clear View Once toggle or button. The user can enable or disable View Once before sending. The default must be normal media (View Once off) — the user must explicitly opt in to View Once.

FR-02  P0

Clear View Once Indicator Before Sending

When View Once is enabled, the UI must clearly indicate that the media will be viewable only once. This includes a label or icon showing "View Once," a brief explanation ("This photo/video will be viewable once and then deleted"), and confirmation that the sender understands before sending.

FR-03  P0

View Once Media Sent with Chat Indicator

When View Once media is sent, it must appear in the chat with a clear indicator (e.g., "View Once" label or circular icon with "1"). Both sender and recipient can see this indicator before the media is opened.

FR-04  P0

Recipient Can Open View Once Media Only Once

When a recipient taps View Once media: the media opens in a fullscreen or dedicated viewer, the media is marked as "opened" internally, and after viewing, the media becomes permanently inaccessible and cannot be opened again.

FR-05  P0

Placeholder After View Once Media Is Opened

After the recipient opens View Once media, the chat must show a placeholder instead of the media (e.g., "View Once media has been opened"). The original media is no longer visible or accessible to anyone.

FR-06  P0

No Saving to Gallery

View Once media must not be saved to the recipient's device gallery or photo library. The system must prevent both automatic and manual saving of the media to persistent storage on the recipient's device.

FR-07  P0

No Forwarding or Downloading

The Forward and Download options must be disabled for View Once media. The recipient cannot share View Once media with others or save it manually through any in-app mechanism.

FR-08  P0

Screenshots Blocked

While viewing View Once media, screenshots must be blocked at the system level. The platform should prevent screenshot capture using secure window flags on Android or equivalent protections on iOS.

FR-09  P0

Screen Recording Blocked

While viewing View Once media, screen recording must also be blocked. The system should detect and prevent active screen recording when View Once media is open and visible on screen.

FR-10  P0

Support for 1:1 Chats and Group Chats

View Once media must work in both 1:1 chats (single recipient can view once) and group chats (each participant can view the media once independently). Each participant's view status must be tracked separately by the system.

NFR-06CompatibilityFeature must work on Android 8+, iOS 14+, and Web/Desktop with consistent UX and no critical functionality missing on any supported platform

Section 11

Assumptions & Constraints

Assumptions:

  • Recipients who receive View Once media have WhatsApp installed and are active users; for recipients without WhatsApp, media will not be delivered (standard WhatsApp behavior)
  • Both sender and recipient have internet access when sending and viewing View Once media; the feature is not designed for fully offline use in v1
  • Users will read the explanation before sending and understand that View Once media can be viewed once and cannot be saved, forwarded, or screenshotted

Constraints:

  • Images and videos only in v1 — voice notes, documents, and other file types are not supported as View Once
  • Behavior is fixed: view once, then inaccessible, users cannot choose how long the recipient can view the media; timers or custom expiry are out of scope for v1

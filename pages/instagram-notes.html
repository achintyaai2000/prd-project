Section 02

Executive Summary

Instagram risks losing its younger audience to TikTok, a video first platform with algorithm driven discovery and engaging short form content. Traditional photo centric Instagram is not enough to compete with TikTok's viral, entertainment driven experience. Without a response, Instagram faces declining engagement, reduced growth, and potential loss of its core demographic.

This PRD proposes Instagram Reels: a short form video feature within Instagram that allows users to create, share, and discover 15 second multi-clip videos with music, effects, and timer controls. Reels will be powered by algorithm driven discovery, creator monetization, and cross posting capabilities.

Key capabilities (v1)

  • Create short videos with music, effects, and editing tools
  • Algorithm driven Reels feed, discovery beyond just followers
  • Creator monetization via rewards, brand partnerships, and future ad revenue sharing
  • Cross post Reels to Facebook and share externally
  • Retain and grow Instagram's younger audience against TikTok

Section 03

Problem Statement

The Core Problem: Instagram is losing its younger audience to TikTok. TikTok's short form, algorithm driven video experience is more engaging than Instagram's photo centric, follower based model, leading to lower daily time spent, reduced ad revenue potential, and risk of becoming a secondary platform for younger users.

Who Has This Problem?

  • Younger users (Gen Z, Millennials) who find Instagram's photo centric experience less engaging than TikTok
  • Creators and content producers for whom TikTok offers better discovery and monetization for video content
  • Instagram as a platform losing its younger demographic and facing declining growth
  • Advertisers and brands whose ad reach diminishes as users migrate to TikTok

Evidence of the Problem

SignalData Point
TikTok's rapid growthTikTok reached 1 billion+ monthly users by 2020; Gen Z adoption significantly higher than Instagram; daily time spent growing faster
Instagram engagement declineEngagement among younger users declining on photo centric content; IGTV and Stories have not matched TikTok's engagement
Creator migrationCreators moving to TikTok for better discovery and monetization; viral video content increasingly hosted on TikTok
Competitive pressureTikTok's algorithm driven discovery is more effective than Instagram's follower based model; no dedicated short form video feature on Instagram

What Happens Without Reels?

  • Younger users migrate to TikTok entirely; engagement and content diversity on Instagram decline
  • Creators leave for better discovery and monetization on TikTok; video content shifts away from Instagram
  • Instagram loses younger audience, faces declining growth and relevance, and sees reduced ad revenue
  • Advertisers shift spend to TikTok; brand engagement on Instagram drops

Section 07

User Journey & User Stories

Primary User Journey - Creator

  1. User taps the Reels icon in Instagram navigation; Reels camera opens
  2. User records a 15 second multi-clip video and adds music, effects, timer, and speed controls
  3. User edits clips, adds text, adjusts timing, and previews the final Reel
  4. User adds caption, hashtags, and share settings; publishes to feed and Reels tab
  5. Eligible creators earn via rewards program and brand partnership opportunities

Primary User Journey - Viewer

  1. User taps the Reels icon; algorithm driven Reels feed opens
  2. User watches vertical 15 second videos that autoplay seamlessly
  3. User discovers Reels from creators they don't follow, recommended by interests
  4. User likes, comments, shares, saves, or follows new creators directly from Reels

Section 08

Functional Requirements

FR-01  P0

Reels Camera & Multi-Clip Recording

Users must be able to open a dedicated Reels camera and record multiple clips up to a combined 15-second maximum. The creation flow must be completable in ≤ 5 taps from the Instagram home screen.

FR-02  P0

Licensed Music Library

The system must provide a searchable library of licensed songs for use in Reels. Music must comply with publisher licensing agreements. No unauthorized or uncleared audio may be surfaced to users.

FR-03  P0

Effects & AR Tools

Users must be able to apply visual effects and augmented reality filters during or after recording. The effects library must load within ≤ 2 seconds and be browsable within the creation flow.

FR-04  P0

Timer & Speed Controls

Users must be able to set a countdown timer before recording begins and adjust playback speed (slow motion and fast forward). Controls must be accessible within the primary recording interface.

FR-05  P0

Clip Editing & Text Overlays

Users must be able to trim individual clips, reorder them, and add text overlays with customizable position and timing. A preview of the final Reel must be available before publishing.

FR-06  P0

Algorith Driven Reels Feed

The system must surface Reels from accounts the user does not follow, based on interest signals and engagement history. At least 60% of Reels shown must come from non-followed accounts to drive discovery.

FR-07  P0

Cross-Post to Facebook

Users must be able to cross-post their Reels to Facebook with a single toggle at publish time. Cross-post rate must be tracked as a KPI to measure inter-platform engagement.

FR-08  P0

Creator Monetization, Rewards Program

Eligible creators must be able to enroll in and track earnings from the Reels rewards program. Eligibility criteria (follower count, view thresholds, compliance) must be clearly communicated within the creator dashboard.

Section 10

Assumptions & Constraints

Assumptions:

  • Users will create and watch Reels regularly once the feature is available
  • Music licensing agreements can be secured with major publishers before launch
  • The algorithm will drive effective content discovery with sufficient early data signals

Constraints:

  • 15 second video maximum in v1 - longer Reels (30 sec) are v2
  • Mobile first launch - no desktop support in v1
  • Music licensing agreements will limit song availability at launch
  • Monetization eligibility requirements will reduce the initial creator pool

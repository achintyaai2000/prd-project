Section 02

Executive Summary

Google Maps already helps billions of users navigate to their destination. However, once a user decides to drive, one of the most frequent real world decisions they face is where to fuel up and at what price. Today, users must exit Maps, open a separate app (GasBuddy, Petrowatch, etc.), compare prices, and mentally plan a detour, breaking their navigation flow entirely.

This PRD defines the Live Fuel Price Finder feature for Google Maps, which will surface real time fuel prices from nearby stations inline within the navigation experience, eliminating the need to leave the app, saving users money, and significantly increasing Maps session time and engagement.

Section 03

Problem Statement

The Core Problem: Fuel prices fluctuate significantly, sometimes by ₹3-5/litre (or $0.30-0.50/gallon) across stations within a 2 km radius. Users have no easy, in context way to compare prices while planning or during a drive.

Who Has This Problem?

  • Daily commuters who refuel 2 to 4 times a month and are price sensitive
  • Long distance drivers (highway travelers, truck operators) for whom fuel is a significant trip cost
  • Ride share drivers (Uber, Ola, Rapido) for whom fuel is a direct operating expense
  • Fleet managers managing cost efficiency across multiple vehicles

Evidence of the Problem:

SignalData Point
GasBuddy downloads150M+ globally, clear demand for this information
Maps UXR Survey (2025)68% of Maps users said they would use this if it existed
Google Search data23% of fuel related searches contain "near me" or "cheapest"
Competitive signalApple Maps launched a basic version in the US in 2024 to strong reception

What Happens If We Don't Solve It?

  • Users continue breaking their Maps session to use third party apps like GasBuddy
  • Maps loses session time and engagement to competitors who offer this feature
  • Ride share and commercial drivers: a high DAU, high frequency segment, have weaker lock in to Maps over time
  • Apple Maps, which already has a basic version live in the US, gains a meaningful feature differentiator

User Stories

Epic 1 — Fuel Price Discovery

US-01 · P0 — As a driver, I want to see fuel prices for nearby stations on my Maps home screen so I can make a quick decision before starting my trip.
US-02 · P0 — As a driver, I want fuel stations to be sorted by price (cheapest first) so I don't have to manually compare.
US-03 · P1 — As a driver, I want to see when a price was last updated so I can trust whether it's current.
US-04 · P1 — As a driver, I want to filter by fuel type (petrol / diesel / CNG / EV) so I only see relevant prices.
US-05 · P2 — As a driver, I want to see a price trend (↑↓ vs yesterday) so I can decide to refuel now or wait.

Epic 2 — In-Navigation Fuel Alerts

US-06 · P0 — As a long distance driver on active navigation, I want a proactive prompt ("Cheap fuel in 3 km — ₹94.5/L") so I can decide to stop without searching.
US-07 · P0 — As a driver, I want to add a fuel station as a waypoint mid navigation with one tap so my route updates automatically.
US-08 · P1 — As a driver, I want to see the detour time added by a fuel stop so I can make an informed decision.

Epic 3 — Station Detail & Community

US-09 · P1 — As a driver, I want to see a station's operating hours on the fuel card so I don't navigate to a closed station.
US-10 · P1 — As a driver, I want to report an incorrect price so the data stays accurate for the community.
US-11 · P2 — As a driver, I want to see whether a station has a convenience store, restroom, or car wash so I can plan multi stop breaks.
Section 08

Functional Requirements

FR-01  P0

Fuel Price Data Ingestion

The system shall ingest real time fuel price data from verified partner sources (IOCL API, HPCL API, third party aggregators). Data must be refreshed at minimum every 4 hours per station. Each record must contain: station ID, fuel type, price per litre, timestamp, and source.

FR-02  P0

Fuel Station Surface on Maps Home

A "Fuel Prices" chip or card shall appear on the Maps home screen for users who have driving as their default travel mode, or who have recently searched for fuel. Tapping it opens a list of up to 10 nearby stations sorted by price.

FR-03  P0

Price Sort & Filter

The fuel station list must support: Sort by Price (default), Distance, Rating. Filter by fuel type: Petrol, Diesel, CNG, EV. Filter by: Open now, Within X km radius.

FR-04  P0

Route Aware Fuel Suggestions During Navigation

During active navigation, the system shall identify fuel stations within 500m of the route and surface up to 3 suggestions as a non intrusive bottom sheet card. The card must show: station name, fuel price, distance from current location, and detour time added.

FR-05  P0

One Tap Waypoint Addition

From any fuel station suggestion card, the user shall be able to add the station as a waypoint with a single tap. The navigation route must update within 3 seconds of tapping.

FR-06  P1

Price Freshness Indicator

Every price displayed must show a freshness label: "Updated 2 hrs ago", "Updated today", or "Price unverified" if data is older than 12 hours. Stale prices (>12 hours) shall be visually distinguished with grey text and an asterisk.

FR-07  P1

User Price Reporting

Users shall be able to report a price discrepancy from a station's detail page. The form collects: correct price, fuel type, and optionally a photo. Reports must pass through the automated + human review queue within 2 hours.

FR-08  P2

Price Trend Indicator

Each station listing shall show a trend indicator: ↑ (higher than 24h ago), ↓ (lower), → (unchanged). Clicking the indicator shows a 7 day mini price chart for that station.

FR-09  P1

Fuel Type Personalization

The system shall remember the user's fuel type preference (set during first use or in Settings) and default to showing that fuel type across all fuel related surfaces without requiring re-selection.

FR-10  P1

Offline Graceful Degradation

If the user is offline or data is unavailable, fuel price features shall gracefully hide rather than crash. The last cached prices (up to 6 hours old) may be shown with a clear "Last known price" label.

Section 11

Assumptions & Constraints

Assumptions:

  • Fuel price data partnerships can be finalized in target markets before launch
  • Users in target markets have Android 9+ or iOS 14+ devices
  • Crowdsourced price reports will maintain accuracy with automated moderation
  • Data partner APIs will maintain <4 hour refresh cycles as contractually agreed

Constraints:

  • Timeline: MVP (P0 features) must ship within Q3 2026 to pre-empt Apple Maps expansion
  • Data partnership costs must not exceed budget threshold defined by Finance team
  • In EU markets, price display must comply with consumer pricing transparency laws
  • In markets without data partners, feature launches with crowdsourced only data

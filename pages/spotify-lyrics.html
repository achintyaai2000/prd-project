Section 02

Executive Summary

Users frequently leave Spotify to search for lyrics on other apps or websites (e.g., Genius, Google) because understanding lyrics helps them connect more deeply with songs. Many listeners enjoy singing along, and non-native speakers use lyrics to better understand songs in other languages. Competitors were already offering integrated lyrics experiences, creating a meaningful competitive gap.

This PRD proposes In-App Real Time Lyrics for Spotify, a feature that displays song lyrics synchronized with the music being played, allowing users to follow along as the song progresses. With In-App Lyrics, lyrics appear in real time scrolling or highlighting as the song plays, users can view lyrics without leaving the app, lyrics support multiple languages and scripts (Hindi, Japanese, Arabic, Korean, etc.), and users can tap any line to jump to that timestamp in the song.

This feature is designed to reduce friction, increase engagement, improve retention, and strengthen Spotify's position as a complete music experience platform.

Key Capabilities (v1)

  • Real time synchronized lyrics display
  • Lyrics scrolling or line highlighting as song plays
  • Tap any line to jump functionality
  • Support for multiple languages and scripts
  • Lyrics availability indicator (if lyrics exist for a song)
  • Basic search within lyrics (if prioritized)
Section 03

Problem Statement

The Core Problem: Users want to understand or sing along to songs but currently have no integrated way to view lyrics within Spotify. This forces them to leave the app to search on Google, Genius, or lyric websites; interrupting their listening experience and missing the opportunity to connect deeply with the song. This leads to increased friction in discovering song meaning, interrupted listening sessions, and lower competitive parity with other music platforms.

Who Has This Problem?

  • Casual music listeners — want to understand what the artist is saying; frequently search lyrics for favorite songs outside Spotify, breaking their listening session
  • Karaoke enthusiasts — want to sing along while listening; don't remember all the lyrics and need a real time reference without a separate app
  • Language learners — use music to improve vocabulary and pronunciation; miss words when listening to fast paced songs in a foreign language
  • Music fans & analysts — interested in songwriting and deeper meaning; cannot fully appreciate or analyze a song without accurate in context lyrics
  • Accessibility users — users with hearing difficulties who benefit from reading lyrics while listening; hard to follow songs without text support

Evidence of the Problem:

SignalData Point
User behavior dataHigh volume of users leaving Spotify to search for lyrics; increased session drop off when users search for lyrics externally
User feedbackFrequent requests for "in-app lyrics," "real time lyrics," and "lyrics synchronized with music" across app store reviews and support tickets
Competitive contextCompetitors like Apple Music, Genius, and YouTube Music already offer integrated lyrics; Spotify lacks a native lyrics experience, creating a clear competitive gap

What Happens If We Don't Solve It?

  • Users continue switching to Google, Genius, or lyric websites; spending time outside the Spotify ecosystem and interrupting their listening experience
  • Missed engagement opportunities; Spotify loses session time and repeat play potential to the friction of external lyric searching
  • Lower competitive parity; Apple Music, YouTube Music, and Genius continue to offer a better integrated lyrics experience, risking user churn to these platforms

Section 08

Functional Requirements

FR-01  P0

Lyrics Indicator on Song Play Screen

When a song is playing, if lyrics exist, a lyrics icon (e.g., a microphone icon or "Lyrics" label) must appear on the play screen. Tapping it opens the lyrics view. If lyrics do not exist, the icon must not appear or must show a disabled state.

FR-02  P0

Real-Time Synchronized Lyrics Display

Lyrics must appear synchronized with the music, highlighting or scrolling as the song plays. Timestamp accuracy must be ≤1 second latency between the audio and the displayed lyrics at all times during playback.

FR-03  P0

Tap Any Line to Jump Functionality

Listeners must be able to tap any lyric line to jump to that timestamp in the song. The song must resume playing from that exact point immediately, with lyrics synchronization maintained from the new position.

FR-04  P0

Multi Language and Script Support

Lyrics must support multiple languages and scripts including Hindi (Devanagari), Japanese (Hiragana, Katakana, Kanji), Arabic, Korean (Hangul), and all other major scripts. Font rendering must be correct and legible for all supported scripts.

FR-05  P0

Lyrics Availability Message

If lyrics are not available for a song, a clear and friendly message must appear (e.g., "Lyrics not available for this song") so users understand the limitation without frustration. The message must not appear as an error.

FR-06  P0

Cross Platform Lyrics Support

Lyrics must work on Mobile (Android and iOS), Web, and Desktop (Windows and macOS). The UX must be consistent across all platforms with no critical functionality missing on any supported platform.

FR-07  P0

Lyrics Loading Time

Lyrics must load within ≤2 seconds on average devices and networks from the moment the user taps the lyrics icon. Poor loading time reduces user trust and breaks the emotional moment of engaging with a song.

FR-08  P0

Lyrics Click Event Tracking

The system must track which lines are tapped and how often lyrics are opened per session. This data must feed into analytics dashboards and optimization efforts to improve lyrics quality, coverage, and user experience.

Section 09

Non-Functional Requirements

NFR IDCategoryRequirement
NFR-01PerformanceLyrics must appear at the correct timestamp with ≤1 second latency on average devices and networks at all times during playback
NFR-02ConsistencyLyrics must behave consistently across Mobile (Android, iOS), Web, and Desktop; no critical functionality missing on any platform
NFR-03AccessibilityLyrics must be accessible via keyboard navigation, compatible with screen readers, and clear for users with visual or motor disabilities; WCAG 2.1 AA compliance required
NFR-04ReliabilitySystem must handle network failures gracefully, show clear error messages if lyrics fail to load, and avoid crashes when opening or interacting with lyrics view
NFR-05ScalabilitySystem must support high concurrent usage of lyrics during peak hours; backend must scale without performance degradation
NFR-06AvailabilityLyrics feature must maintain ≥99.5% uptime during operational hours; critical failures detected and mitigated quickly
NFR-07ObservabilitySystem must log key events (lyrics opened, line tapped, sync error) and provide monitoring dashboards for lyrics open rate, sync accuracy, and error rates
NFR-08PrivacyLyrics data must comply with Spotify's privacy policies and applicable regulations (e.g., GDPR); no sensitive user data exposed via the lyrics feature
NFR-09LicensingLyrics must comply with licensing agreements with publishers and lyric providers; all legal requirements for displaying lyrics must be met; no unauthorized lyrics displayed
Section 10

Design & UX Requirements

Entry Points:

Entry PointDescription
Play ScreenLyrics icon appears on the play screen when a song is playing and lyrics exist; tapping opens lyrics view
Minimized PlayerLyrics icon may appear in minimized player if prioritized, allowing quick access without opening full play screen

Core UX Principles:

  • Clear visibility of lyrics — always easy to find and read
  • Easy navigation — accessible in ≤2 taps from any playback state
  • Real time synchronization — lyrics match the music at all times
  • Cross-platform consistency — same experience on mobile, web, and desktop
  • Accessibility-friendly — keyboard navigation, screen reader support
  • Graceful error handling — clear messages when lyrics are unavailable or fail to load

Tone & Messaging Guidelines:

  • Clear and simple — avoid jargon like "synchronized lyric data" or "timestamped text stream"
  • Engaging — emphasize connection with music and the joy of singing along
  • Transparent — explain clearly if lyrics are not available for a song

Section 11

Assumptions & Constraints

Assumptions:

  • Lyrics providers will supply accurate, timestamped lyrics for the songs in Spotify's catalog
  • Users have internet connectivity when listening and accessing lyrics
  • All target platforms (Android, iOS, Web, Desktop) support multi-language font rendering for all required scripts

Constraints:

  • Lyrics must be licensed; legal agreements with publishers and lyric providers are required before displaying any lyrics
  • Not all songs have lyrics; coverage will vary by catalog and market; unavailability must be communicated gracefully
  • Multi language support requires significant localization effort; font rendering, right-to-left scripts (Arabic), and character encoding must all be handled correctly
  • Sync accuracy is dependent on the data quality provided by the lyrics partner; poor source data will affect the user experience
